8. Specific Accounting Problem

KK was buying back franchises. Common sense says this should cost KK, however due to the way they were booking it the purchases were a revenue source.

8.1. Franchise Purchase Deal

  • Franchisee closes stores and pays KK (corporate) accrued interest on past-due loans.
  • This was recorded on the income statement as interest income. <— we are fine at this point.
  • The purchase cost of the franchise was booked as an intangible asset (reacquired franchise rights).
  • The implication is that the purchase price is not amortized, meaning it doesn't show up on the income statement.
  • Also severance payments were not amortized.
  • Net effect, buying back franchises increases income.

This treatment was fine by GAAP, but was not used by any other firms.

  • So did the market think the company was using this accounting approach?
  • If not, what effect does this have on asymmetric information? What about on stock volatility?
